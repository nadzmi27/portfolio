# Introduction

I first got introduced to chess in 2015 by a friend.
I lived in a boarding school in Malaysia, and everyday I would play chess with him and some other friends.
I wasn't able to win a single match against him for 3 months straight, but on one faithful day, I took my first win.
From that day onwards, I was able to consistently win against him, and started challenging people who are more skilled.
I became obsessed with chess, and decided to join the school competition.

The game was very popular amongst the students, and almost everyone knows how to play, so naturally the competition would be fierce.
So I trained rigorously for a couple weeks by solving chess puzzle via mobile app (<a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=net.lrstudios.android.chess_problems" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Chess Tactics Pro</a>).
On the day of the competition, I performed better than I expected and managed to place within top 10 and proceed to the next stage.
The next stage would be inter-school competition, and if I were to place well, I would make it to the state competition.

Unfortunately, on the day of the competition, I was outperformed by the other competitors.
I was just 0.5 points off to qualify to next level, and instead of feeling disheartened, I feel inspired as I know I can do better given that I'm new to the game.
Unfortunately, my love for chess got left behind when I had to move to Australia. I still play chess once in a blue moon, but it was just for fun.

Fast forward to this year (2026), my love for chess got rekindled, and I became obsessed with it again.
Then I saw an Instagram post on inter-uni competition, and was hesitant whether to join or not.
Fortunately, my friend pushed me to join, and even pay the entry fee for me.
